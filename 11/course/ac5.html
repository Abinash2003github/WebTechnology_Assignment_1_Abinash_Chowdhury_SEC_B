Bachelor of Arts in Political Science

Course Description

A Bachelor of Arts in Political Science is a four-year undergraduate degree designed to provide students with a deep understanding of political systems, theories, and institutions. This program typically covers areas such as:

  • Political Theory: The study of classical and contemporary political ideas and philosophies.
  • Comparative Politics: Analysis of different political systems and governance structures worldwide.
  • International Relations: The study of global political interactions, including diplomacy, conflict, and cooperation.
  • Public Administration: The principles and practices of managing public sector organizations.
  • Political Economy: The study of the relationship between politics and economics on a national and international scale.
  • Constitutional Law: Examination of legal frameworks that govern political institutions and individual rights.
  • Political Sociology: The analysis of social forces and their influence on political behavior and institutions.
  • Public Policy: The study of policy-making processes and the implementation of governmental decisions.
