Bachelor of Commerce (B.Com)

Course Description

A Bachelor of Commerce (B.Com) is a four-year undergraduate degree designed to provide students with a thorough understanding of various aspects of commerce and business. This program typically covers areas such as:

  • Financial Accounting: Principles and practices of accounting, including financial statements and reporting.
  • Business Law: Legal principles affecting business operations, including contracts, liability, and regulations.
  • Management Principles: Basic concepts of management, organizational behavior, and strategic planning.
  • Economics: Microeconomics and macroeconomics principles relevant to business decisions.
  • Marketing: Strategies and techniques for marketing products and services effectively.
  • Business Statistics: Statistical methods and tools for analyzing business data.
  • Financial Management: Techniques for managing financial resources, including budgeting, investment, and risk management.
  • Human Resource Management: Concepts related to recruiting, managing, and developing employees.
