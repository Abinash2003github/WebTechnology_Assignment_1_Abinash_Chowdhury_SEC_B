Bachelor of Arts in Sociology

Course Description

A Bachelor of Arts in Sociology is a four-year undergraduate degree designed to provide students with a thorough understanding of social behavior, social institutions, and societal dynamics. This program typically covers areas such as:

  • Introduction to Sociology: The foundational concepts and theories of sociology, including social structures and cultural norms.
  • Social Theory: Major theoretical frameworks and perspectives used to analyze social phenomena.
  • Research Methods: Techniques for conducting social research, including qualitative and quantitative methods.
  • Social Institutions: The study of institutions such as family, education, religion, and government.
  • Gender Studies: Exploration of gender roles, identities, and inequalities in society.
  • Urban Sociology: The examination of social dynamics and issues within urban environments.
  • Deviance and Social Control: Analysis of behavior that deviates from social norms and the mechanisms of social control.
  • Globalization and Social Change: The impact of globalization on societies and the processes of social change.
