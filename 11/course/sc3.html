Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering

Course Description

A Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering is a four-year undergraduate degree designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of civil engineering principles. This program typically covers areas such as:

  • Structural Analysis: The study of structures and their behavior under various loads.
  • Geotechnical Engineering: The study of soil and rock behavior and its interaction with structures.
  • Transportation Engineering: The design and operation of transportation systems and infrastructure.
  • Environmental Engineering: The development of solutions for environmental issues and sustainable practices.
  • Hydraulics and Hydrology: The study of water flow and its management in civil engineering projects.
  • Construction Management: Techniques and practices for managing construction projects.
  • Concrete and Steel Design: The design and analysis of concrete and steel structures.
  • Surveying: Techniques for measuring and mapping land and construction sites.
