Bachelor of Arts in Psychology

Course Description

A Bachelor of Arts in Psychology is a four-year undergraduate degree designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of human behavior, mental processes, and psychological research methods. This program typically covers areas such as:

  • Introduction to Psychology: An overview of the fundamental principles of psychology, including cognitive, developmental, and social psychology.
  • Developmental Psychology: The study of psychological growth and change across the lifespan.
  • Cognitive Psychology: An examination of mental processes such as memory, perception, and problem-solving.
  • Abnormal Psychology: The study of psychological disorders and their treatment.
  • Social Psychology: The exploration of how individuals are influenced by social interactions and group dynamics.
  • Research Methods in Psychology: Techniques and methodologies for conducting psychological research, including experiments and surveys.
  • Biopsychology: The study of the biological underpinnings of behavior and mental processes.
  • Personality Psychology: An exploration of the theories and research surrounding personality development and individual differences.
