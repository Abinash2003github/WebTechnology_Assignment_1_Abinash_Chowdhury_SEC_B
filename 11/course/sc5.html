Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering

Course Description

A Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering is a four-year undergraduate degree designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of computer engineering principles. This program typically covers areas such as:

  • Digital Logic Design: The study of logic gates, circuits, and systems used in computing devices.
  • Microprocessors and Microcontrollers: The principles and applications of processors in embedded systems.
  • Computer Architecture: The design and organization of computer systems and their components.
  • Operating Systems: The study of software that manages computer hardware and software resources.
  • Data Structures and Algorithms: The study of efficient ways to store and manipulate data.
  • Software Engineering: The principles and practices of designing, developing, and testing software.
  • Networking: The study of computer networks, protocols, and communication systems.
  • Database Systems: The design and management of data storage and retrieval systems.
