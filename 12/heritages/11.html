Ninna-ji Temple

Ninna-ji Temple, established in 888 by the Emperor Uda, is a significant Buddhist temple in Kyoto, Japan. Known for its impressive five-story pagoda and beautiful gardens, Ninna-ji Temple is a UNESCO World Heritage site. The temple complex includes a main hall, various pagodas, and the serene Kannon-do Hall, dedicated to the Buddhist deity of compassion. Ninna-ji Temple is celebrated for its annual cherry blossom festival and the iconic cherry trees that bloom spectacularly in spring.
