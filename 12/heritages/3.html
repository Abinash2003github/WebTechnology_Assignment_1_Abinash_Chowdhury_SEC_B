Kiyomizu-dera Temple

Kiyomizu-dera, also known as Kiyomizu Temple, is a historic temple in Kyoto, Japan. Founded in the late 8th century, this Buddhist temple is renowned for its stunning wooden stage that juts out over the hillside, offering panoramic views of the city and the surrounding cherry and maple trees. The temple is named after the Otowa Waterfall that runs beneath its main hall, with the water considered to have healing properties. Kiyomizu-dera is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a popular destination for both tourists and locals.
