Fushimi Inari Taisha

Fushimi Inari Taisha is a renowned Shinto shrine located in Kyoto, Japan, famous for its thousands of vibrant red torii gates that create a stunning path up the sacred Mount Inari. Founded in the 8th century, the shrine is dedicated to Inari, the Shinto deity of rice and agriculture. Visitors can hike through the tunnel of torii gates, which lead to the shrine’s main sanctuary at the mountain’s summit. The site is one of Kyoto's most iconic and visited landmarks, offering breathtaking views and a unique spiritual experience.
