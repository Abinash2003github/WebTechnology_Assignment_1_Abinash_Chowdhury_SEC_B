Gion District

The Gion District is a historic neighborhood in Kyoto, Japan, renowned for its traditional wooden machiya houses, tea houses, and geisha culture. Established in the 17th century, Gion is one of Kyoto's most famous districts, offering a glimpse into the city's rich cultural heritage. Visitors can stroll along the picturesque streets of Hanamikoji and Shirakawa, where they may encounter geisha and maiko (apprentice geisha) in their traditional attire. Gion is also known for its traditional festivals, such as the Gion Matsuri, which takes place every July.

Visitor Information

Address: Gion, Higashiyama-ku, Kyoto 605-0001, Japan

Opening Hours: The district is open year-round; specific attractions and tea houses may have varying hours.

Admission Fee: Free to explore the district; individual attractions and tea houses may have entry fees.

Getting There: Accessible via Kyoto City Bus #100 or #206, get off at Gion bus stop.
