Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)

Kinkaku-ji, also known as the Golden Pavilion, is a stunning Zen Buddhist temple in Kyoto, Japan. Originally built as a retirement villa for the shogun Ashikaga Yoshimitsu, it was converted into a temple after his death. The temple's top two floors are covered in gold leaf, making it one of the most iconic and beautiful sites in Kyoto.

Visitor Information

Address: 1 Kinkakuji-cho, Kita-ku, Kyoto 603-8361, Japan

Opening Hours: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM (last entry at 4:30 PM)

Admission Fee: ¥400 for adults, ¥300 for children

Getting There: Accessible via Kyoto City Bus #101 or #205, get off at Kinkaku-ji-michi bus stop.
