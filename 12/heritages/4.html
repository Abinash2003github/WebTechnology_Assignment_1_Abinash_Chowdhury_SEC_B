Nijo Castle

Nijo Castle is a historic castle located in Kyoto, Japan, built in 1603 by Tokugawa Ieyasu, the founder of the Tokugawa shogunate. The castle served as the shogun's residence during his visits to Kyoto and is renowned for its beautiful gardens, ornate interiors, and unique architectural features. The Ninomaru Palace within the castle grounds is particularly famous for its "nightingale floors," which chirp when walked upon, serving as an anti-intruder measure. Nijo Castle is a UNESCO World Heritage site and an important cultural landmark.
